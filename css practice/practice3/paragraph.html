Ozymandias

by percy bysshe shelley

I met a traceller from an antique land,
who said_"Two vast and trunkless legs of stone
stand in the desert . . . .Near them,on the sand,
Half sunk a shattered visage lies,whose frown,
And wrinked lip,and sneer of cold command,
Tell that its sculptor well those passions read
Which yet surview,stamped on these lifeless things,
And on the pedastal,these world apper:
my name is Ozymandias,king of kings;
Look on my works ye Mighty,and despire!
Nothing beside remains.Round the dency
of that colossakl wreck,boundless and bare
The lone and level sands stretch far away."
